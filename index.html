AMR faults on another P&D rack - Troubleshooting

ISSUE: An AMR will occasionally take an abnormal path through the dock area to get to its destination. There are certain spots where the AMR scanners will fault on another P&D rack if it rotates while it has a load on it.

Step 1: Connect to the AMR with the brake release.
Step 2: Rotate the AMR so that it is facing forwards on the path it is trying to take.
Step 3: Switch the AMR back into auto mode.

Step 1: Use KUKA Studio or the manual controller to back the AMR out to the approach node for the P&D rack.
Step 2: When the AMR is back in Auto mode, the AMR may immediately continue with its docking mission.
Step 3: If the AMR does not continue:
3a. In KUKA AMR Fleet, go to AMR Info for that AMR.
3b.Click Advanced Debugging.
3c.Click Retry.

How to brake release the AMR

Step 1: Switch the key on the back of the AMR to the far-right position.
Step 2: Make sure that the reset button is not flashing. Press it to turn it solid if it is.
Step 3:To disengage the brakes, press the start button on the AMR. There will be a click from the AMR when the brakes release. The AMR can now be pushed to the desired position.
Step 4: Make sure that the final position of the AMR is on top of a node on the graph.
Step 5: When the AMR is in the desired position, switch the key back to the far-left position in auto mode.
Step 6: If the Reset or Start buttons are flashing, press them to turn them solid.The AMR should appear online after a few seconds and its position should update on the map.

AMR Safety Guidelines

Travel Direction: The AMRs will follow the ‚Äúrules of the road‚Äù and drive on the right side of the hallways, sharing the same lane as forklifts.
Intersection: When going through an intersection, the AMR will slow down to 1 m/s (2.2 mph) but will NOT stop.
Warning Light: Rotating beacon lights will notify forklift operators when an AMR is about to cross into the intersection. Forklifts must yield to AMRs entering an intersection. Beacon lights will be placed at the at the areas circled in red.

FLASHING GREENüü¢ AMR is in motion. Stay clear of its path; it may not stop in time if you cut in front.
SOLID GREENüü¢: AMR is stationary.
FLASHING YELLOW/WHITEüü°: AMR is in its docking process.Safety field is reduced, therefore avoid standing directly in front of or behind the AMR ‚Äî it won‚Äôt detect people or obstacles close to it during docking.
REDüî¥: Safety scanners are faulted. Check for any object blocking the scan field. Remove the obstruction, and the AMR will resume after a few seconds.
PURPLEüü£ : Cameras are detecting an obstacle.Stop and check the area ‚Äî Remove the obstacle, and the AMR will resume after a few seconds.
LIGHTS OFF : AMR is idle and waiting for next mission.
360¬∞ Detection: Safety scanners and 3D cameras on the front and rear provide full obstacle coverage around the AMR.

Detection Height Limit: Scanners only detect objects exactly 6.5 inches above the ground ‚Äî anything higher or lower is not detected


Automatic Stop: If an object enters the scan field, the AMR automatically stops. It resumes a few seconds after the obstacle is removed.

Scan Field Size During Loaded Largest detection zone. Covers the skid (62‚Äùx92‚Äù) plus 44‚Äù in front and 8‚Äù on each side. AMR drives forward.
Scan Field Size During Unloaded: Smaller zone for the empty AMR. Includes 44‚Äù behind and 8‚Äù on each side. AMR drives backward.
Scan Field Size During Docking/Charging:Detection zone is tight, just slightly larger than the AMR body.
